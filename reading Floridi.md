# Reading Floridi
three interconnected theses concerning the philosophical, ethical, and socio-political implications of the digital and analogue relationship
1. digital representations (models) increasingly mediate our cognitive and practical engagement with reality (systems), resulting in epistemic abstraction and potential oversights (“the eclipse of the analogue”). 
2. at the same time and only apparently paradoxically, analogue factors are becoming central to the control and governance of digital technologies (“the hardware turn”). Despite digitalisation’s rhetoric of immateriality and virtuality, power increasingly resides with those who control the analogue conditions of possibility underpinning the digital domain
3. a normative framework for addressing this twofold challenge.
we increasingly rely on digital _models_ to deal cognitively with analogue _systems_
the map (model) is not the territory (system)
human understanding of any reality is shaped by abstractions
_politically_ and _economically_, we increasingly control digital models not just in terms of their design, development, and delivery but,also in terms of their analogue conditions of possibility
the analogue makes the digital possible
avoid a misleading disconnect between 
(a) our epistemic experience of digital models and 
(b) our practical interactions with analogue systems 
as well as a twofold disempowerment about 
(c) the DDD of models and 
(d) the control of the systems that make them possible
 call “system” any reality in itself that is the referent of our interactions, whether epistemic or not.
 Models are the outcome of a process that is always and inevitably selective and interpretative
 models are the result of _abstraction_
 inevitable abstraction with a non-zero, irreversible loss of information: you cannot reconstruct the full cat only from the model.
 The only models that adequately represent reality are those that declare their limits (what they abstract) and purpose (what they abstract it for).
 when we interact with the world, we know models but experience systems.
  if you build something, that artefact is a system, a noumenon, a new piece of reality in itself, even if you will always know it only through some mediating model
  in Plato, realities as we experience them are implementations (systems) of previous models understood as _blueprints_, i.e., the sources of the to-be-realised systems.
  Kant: our systems are now the _noumena_ (things-in-themselves), and our models are the _phenomena_ (the things-in-themselves as they appear to human cognitive experience)
systems are unknowable not in an _epistemically inaccessible_ sense, but as _epistemically inexhaustible sources_ of data that become information through cognitive processes that generate models
mediation represents a constructive elaboration of signals sent by the noumena, which are not descriptions of them.
Language represents reality in that it models it through logical structure, not in the sense that it directly reflects it.
digital models may prioritise abstract representations (models) over the immediacy of lived experience (systems and their interactions), and a dangerous disconnection from the tangible world may emerge, as we increasingly engage only with mediated, digital environments,
Baudrillard’s models without modelled systems (“copies without originals”) that become self-sufficient, creating their own reality rather than representing something external or authentic.
Approach the system-model distinction from a _mimetic_ perspective
 knowledge as representing the known object, not as making it
 See Horkheimer on how models mediate and obscure systems in a culture industry that standardises and commodifies cultural production.
 Weil provides an insightful analysis of tools as mediators of human labour and experience.
 In _Oppression and Liberty_ (1955), Weil analyzes tools as **mediators of human labour and experience**.
 deracinement as distinct from alientation
 epistemologically, a bias in favour of models rather than systems, is reinforced and accelerated by the enabling opportunities offered by the boundless malleability of the digital when compared to the analogue.
