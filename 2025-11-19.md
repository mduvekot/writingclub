Earliest uses of tool-like words

## utensil 
1. 1411
    
    > Y be-qweythe to lucye my wyfe..alle þe ==vtensyl== of myn hows, þat ys to say, in halle, in Chambre, in Pantrie and Botrie, in larder and Kechyn.
    
    in F. J. Furnivall, Fifty Earliest English Wills (1882) 18

## apparatus

1. 1728
    
    > The Furniture or ==Apparatus== of an Air-pump, Microscope, &c.
    
    E. Chambers, Cyclopædia (at cited word)

## device
1. 1570
    
    > He alone, with his ==deuises== and engynes..spoyled and discomfited the whole Army.
    
    J. Dee in H. Billingsley, translation of Euclid, Elements of Geometrie Math. Præf. sig. dj
# machine
1. 1659
    
    > I caused a ==Machine== to be made of Iron..and being well seated in the seat, I cast my Magnetique Bowl into the Air.
    
    T. St. Serfe, translation of S. de Cyrano de Bergerac, Σεληναρχια sig. D3v
