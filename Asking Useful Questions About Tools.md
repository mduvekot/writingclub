# Things we can ask of a tool: 
## Relationship to the User
* Who uses the tool? Who is the user? (You're the apparent "phantom" user of a weather app, the data broker who is selling that data that the app collected about you is the real user/beneficiary).
* What is required from the user? (do you need skills/training to use the tool? which ones?)
* Do I understand what it does?
* How can I know if/when the tool malfunctions?
* Does it allow me to do something I could not otherwise do?
* How does the tool impact me/its user? (Does using a heavy power-drill cause carpal tunnel syndrome? Does an LLM cause cognitive atrophy?)
* Is the user ceding their autonomy to the tool? Are they reliant on something they do not understand and do not control?
## Relationship to the domain/context
* What is the operand? (What does the tool operate on? Table saws are designed to work on wood) 
* What can it operate on? (table saws work on your fingers as well as lumber)
* In what contexts can it be used? (don't use a screwdriver to open paint cans)
* Who designed the tool? 
* Is the domain in which it operates well-defined? (Is the tool specificity narrow or broad, or does the tool work on "anything"?) 
## Power Structures/Control
* Can the tool be contolled/disabled remotely (by someone who is not the apparent user)?
* Who owns the tool? (Are you able to do whatever you want with it?)
* Are the features/function of the tool fixed? (Can a software company change the way their product works without the user's consent?) 
* Who owns its outputs? (Does Facebook own the copyright to things you created?)
* Does it require maintenance (by whom)? (Can you only get your car serviced at the dealership?)  
* Can it be modified/updated? (Do you have right-to-repair?)
* Does it have guardrails? (Are there things you are not allowed to use it for? Does it have a EULA?)
* Are costs offloaded onto non-users, non-beneficiaries? (Your car gives me cancer)
* Does it cause disruption that imposes limits on its use? (Don't use table saws in a residential area when people would like to sleep).
* Does the tool require an external, proprietary system to function (Give an example of devices that need to contact a service that is no longer provided by the manufacturer, like Nest Learning Thermostats, or Revolvhub)
## Cost
* Does it need to be regularly replaced (A hammer will last you years, a smartphone is outdated and unsupported withing 5-7 years)
* What costs are incurred? (one-time purchase, pay-per-use, licensed, free/libre/gratis,  open source?)
## Ontology
* What type/class of tool is it? (Kinaesthetic? Hermeneutic? (per Ihde))
## Evaluation of objective impacts
 * Does the tool perform predictably? (Given the same inputs, are the outputs the same, or is the output stochastic?) 
* Is there a disconnect/disjunction/gap between what it does and what it was (ostensibly) designed to do?
* Does it improve productivity?
* Does it have side-effects?
* What is the opportunity cost of its use?
* Does it require a novel skill?
* Does it obviate a skill? 
* Can the user validate/check the quality of the output? (Can I tell when the model (LLM) is wrong?) (I can tell that a screwdriver bit it worn by merley looking at it, but also by feel and will likely cam out a screw and damage my work, and when I does, I'll feel it slip).
* What affordances does it offer?
## Legislative  
* Who is liable is the tool produces defective/faulty/undesirable/illegal outputs?
* Do you need to be licensed to operate the tool? (Example: A marine or aviation radio)

## Externalities
* Is the tool vulnerable to sabotage/hacking/attacks?
* Qui bono? Who benefits, who is hurt?
* What is the power structure?
* What are the incentives?
* What is the business model? (Are you buying printers or printer ink? Enshittification?)

# Things we can ask of a tool-answer:

Is this tool fit for my intended purpose in this particular context?
Is there another tool to would work better/cheaper/faster?
Can I reconcile my use of the tool with my values/ethics?
What should I learn? Which skills should I acquire to use the tool appropriately/effectively?
How do I maintain/care for my tool?
Can I trust the tool?
