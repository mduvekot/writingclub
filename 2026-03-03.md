The AT Protocol (Authenticated Transfer Protocol) is a set of open standards for decentralized social apps, where different services (like Bluesky and Gander) can interoperate, and where all published data (text, images, videos) includes built‑in cryptographic proof of who created it.

A user's identity consists of two parts, a human-readable mutable domain name, or #handle that they can change, and an immutable Decentralized IDentifier or #DID that is permanently and cryptographically fixed. This way, users can change services and keep their posts and followers. 

A #repository or #repo is a collection of user’s data: their posts, profile, follows, likes, and other records. A #blob is a special repository for media files. A user's repository is stored on a server that is operated by the service they use, i.e Bluesky or Gander. 

A #repository is the collection of a user’s data: posts, profile, follows, likes, and other records. A #blob is used specifically for media files (images and videos). A user’s repository lives on the server run by the service they use (for example, Bluesky or Gander), which hosts and serves their data on their behalf.

A Personal Data Server (#PDS) is where a user's data (the repositories) are stored and made available. Social Media apps, #clients, like the Gander app access the PDS to fetch data from across the network.

A #relay is a server that gathers, and  republishes data from #PDSes across the network and makes them available in a real-time stream of data called the #firehose.

A #feed is the stream of posts an app shows you, built by a feed generator that uses your public data to select and order content from across the network. When you follow someone, the client writes that record to your PDS, and the feed generator reads it from there to build your “Following” feed.
