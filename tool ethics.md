When you use a tool, does its use impact other people? Have those people consented to that particular use? For example, If I use a hammer in the middle of the night to hammer a nail in the wall that separates my neighbour's bedroom from mine, does that impact them? Would they agree to me hammering at 3AM? Under what conditions? It is possible, if they work a night shift and sleep during the day. Ethical use is use in a normative context. You don't usually make nose in the middle of the night, except when  the context forms an exceptions to convention.

Does my car use affect may neighbour? It certainly does, but they ALSO have a car, so it would be hypocritical of them to object to me using a car if they use a car as well. But 10 cars? Ethical use relies on reciprocity (and proportionality).
